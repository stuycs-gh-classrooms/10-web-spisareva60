Regeneration Phase

In the final stage of the Calvin cycle, regeneration, a small number of G3P molecules are exported to the cytoplasm to perform glycolysis, while others must be recycled to regenerate the RuBP acceptor. More specifically, in order for one G3P to exit the cycle, three molecules must enter the cycle, providing three new atoms of fixed carbon. When three molecules enter the cycle, six G3P molecules are made. One molecule of G3P exits the cycle and is used to make glucose, while the other five must be recycled to regenerate three molecules of the RuBP acceptor.
